 I received a Notice of Change letter in the mail from GVRA. What does this mean?

Notices of Change (NOCs) are official documents used by GVRA to notify individuals of certain decisions and due process rights during the vocational rehabilitation (VR) process. If your Notice of Change includes:

GVRA Policy Cite: 216.1.07 – Congratulations, GVRA has determined you’re eligible for their services! Contact your counselor to schedule your next appointment.

GVRA Policy Cite: 502.1.13 A-F or 502.1.14 A-S GVRA has either determined you’re ineligible for services and/or that an intervening reason exists for case closure. You have 30 days from the receipt of this letter to appeal your case closure.

To appeal, you’ll need to write and submit a letter and/or email (it doesn’t have to be formal) to your counselor with today’s date stating you disagree with your case being closed. Should you submit your appeal in person, take down the name of the person you turned it in to and ask them for a copy for your records.
